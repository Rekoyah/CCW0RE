First Week Coding Terms

Computer

Before it referred to mechanical machines, the word "computer" was described as a human who performed calculations or computations. It was first used in the 1613 book The Yong Mans Gleanings by English poet Richard Braithwaite. By the end of the ninteenth century, "computer" was even an official job title. In honor of Black History Month, American mathematician Katherine Johnson, for example, worked for NASA as a computer. She is widely known for being the only one Alan Shepard, the first American astronaut, trusted to calculate the trajectory for his May 1961 mission Freedom 7.

Difference Engine

The Difference Engine is the first automatic computing machine to calculate polynomials. The D.E. was capable of not only computing several sets of numbers but also providing hard copies of the results. It was conceptualized and began development in 1822 by English mathematician and computer pioneer Charles Babbage. Furthermore, fellow English mathematician Ada Lovelace was considered the first computer programmer for her work on developing the D.E. along with Babbage. Unfortunately though, Babbage never completed a full-scale functional version of the D.E. due to lack of funding. Honoring his contribution, the London Science Museum completed the Difference Engine No. 2 for the bicentennial year of Babbage's birth in June 1991 and later completed the printing mechanism in 2000.

Analytical Engine

The first general mechanical computer, known as the Analytical Engine, was proposed by Charles Babbage in 1837. It was the first general-purpose computer concept developed for many uses and contained components such as basic flow control, integrated memory, and an ALU (Arithmetic Logic Unit). Unfortunately, Babbage couldn't build the A.E. during his lifetime because of funding issues. In 1910, Henry Babbage, his youngest son, built part of the machine and performed basic calculations.

Algorithm

An algorithm is a set of finite rules or instructions to obtain the expected output from the given input. Algorithms play a crucial role in numerous fields such as computer science and mathematics to solve problems of any level.

Version Control

Version Control, also known as source code management, is a system that maintains versions of files at progressive stages of development. Like email or instant messaging, Version Control is a communication tool, but it works with code instead of human conversation. One major benefit of Version Control is it allows a team to share code to optimize collaboration.

Git

Git is one of many distrbuted version control systems designed to handle everything from small to large projects with speed and efficiency. Unlike other version control systems, Git is locally-enabled, which means that you can version control files straight from your desktop.
